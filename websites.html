Web Design Projects

June 2014 - Present

I often design small websites and webpages in my spare time. Everything here was designed and published from scratch using HTML, CSS, PHP, Javascript, and jQuery.

Here are the full-scale websites I have made:

  • MIT Student Theater Website (August 2016, theater.mit.edu): This is a home for information on eight different student-run theater groups at MIT. It includes pages on each group, as well as pages for upcoming events, contact information, and more.
  • Socolovsky Lab Website (July 2014, labs.umassmed.edu/socolovskylab): This is a website for the Socolovsky Lab at the University of Massachusetts Medical School. It includes information on research procedures and goals, as well as group members and publications.

I also work on smaller projects and sites, which are listed below:

  • "Event Predictor": Final Project for Engineering Computation and Data Science (August 2016, web.mit.edu/www/100/termProject): For the final project of the class Engineering Computation and Data Science (1.00), I created this data visualization of NASA's Earth Observatory Natural Event Tracker (EONET). The website plots natural weather events onto a map of the world. Additionally, one page is dedicated to calculating the most likely natrual event to occur in a location. This prediction tool uses the k-nearest-neighbor algorithm on the existing dataset.
  • Dungeons and Dragons Spell List Search (July 2014, web.mit.edu/tals/www/DnDspells5e): Dungeons and Dragons is a Table-Top Role Playing game in which there are many spells with different effects. To make it easier to find the specifics of a spell, I created this webpage to quickly display their descriptions from a search for the title of the spell. The search algorithm is written almost entirely from scratch.
